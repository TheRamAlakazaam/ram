There is still something calming about having one place on the internet that
belongs to you.

Not a profile page. Not a feed. Not an account that can be re-ranked, buried,
or renamed by a company with a quarterly plan. A site is slower than that, and
that is part of the appeal.

I want room for finished things and unfinished things. I want a photograph next
to a dev note, a small list next to a longer essay, and a homepage that can
hold all of it without apologizing for the mix.

That kind of site does not need to be huge. It just needs to be honest, easy to
navigate, and alive enough that returning feels worthwhile.
